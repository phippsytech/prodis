Responsibilities of The Provider:

  • {{PROVIDER_SHORT_NAME}} reviews the provision of supports annually or as needed (such as when circumstances change, a new NDIS plan comes into effect, or participants' needs change, etc.) and is completed with the participant.
  • Once agreed, provide supports that meet the participant's needs at the participant's preferred times.
  • {{PROVIDER_SHORT_NAME}} communicates openly, honestly, and in a timely manner.
  • {{PROVIDER_SHORT_NAME}} has a philosophy and moral obligation to treat the participant with courtesy and respect.
  • Consult the participant on decisions about how supports are provided.
  • Open-door policy for feedback or complaints.
  • {{PROVIDER_SHORT_NAME}} gives the participant information about managing any complaints or disagreements and details of the {{PROVIDER_SHORT_NAME}} cancellation policy.
  • Listen to the participant's feedback and resolve any concerns in a timely manner.
  • Give the participant a maximum or a minimum of 24 hours' notice if {{PROVIDER_SHORT_NAME}} must change a scheduled appointment to provide supports.
  • Give the participant the required notice if {{PROVIDER_SHORT_NAME}} is required to end the service agreement (minimum of two weeks' notice).
  • Protect the participant's privacy and confidential information.
  • Provide supports in a manner consistent with all relevant laws, including the National Disability Insurance Scheme Act 2013 and rules, and the Australian Consumer Law; keep accurate records of the supports provided to the participant, and
  • Issue regular invoices and statements of the supports delivered to the participant.
